\\\ Basic Skills (Hero Tech) ///
Simple core skills every fighter has access to, no matter their class. Basic attacks are rarely the best option, but the basic support abilities can be surprisingly useful.
\\ Max HP 300, 3 TP per turn //
P [P-ATK 100 / P-DEF 100 / P-ACC 100 / P-EVD 100]
R [R-ATK 100 / R-DEF 100 / R-ACC 100 / R-EVD 100]
M [M-ATK 100 / M-DEF 100 / M-ACC 100 / M-EVD 100]
Skills: Fight, Shoot, Spark, First Aid, Use Item, Grab Item, Pass Item, Revive, Wait, Swan Song
Fight (Hero Tech): 110%, P35. A single blow with an improvised technique. In the wise words of the ancients, "Only trust your fists. Police will never help you." (1E, 1 TP)
Shoot (Hero Tech): 110%, R35. A thrown improvised weapon. When in doubt, point and click. (1E, 1 TP)
Spark (Hero Tech): 110%, M35. A blast of improvised magic. Or pyrotechnics. Same thing, really. (1E, 1 TP)
First Aid (Hero Tech): [100%], H30. Patch up an ally with basic arena bandages. (1A/!S, 1 TP)
Use Item (Hero Tech): Crack open a consumable or swing some legendary sword. If you've never seen anything like this weird object before, just wing it! Use an item you're holding. TP cost depends on the item, but is usually 1 TP. Can be used multiple times per turn, as long as you're using different items. (* TP)
Grab Item (Hero Tech): Snag an item to use later. Pick up a single item from the arena floor. Spend 1 TP to take it directly from a willing target's inventory, or snag multiple items from the arena floor. (0 TP)
Pass Item (Hero Tech): Hand off an item. Transfer a single item to a willing target, or drop any number of items to the arena floor. Spend 1 TP to pass any number of items. (0 TP)
Revive (Hero Tech): Offer someone your hand, and get them back on their feet. Clear Downed on an ally, restoring them to 1 HP. (2 TP)
Wait (Hero Tech): Hold your fire for just a little bit longer. Reduce your own CT by any amount up to 50. It's on you to do the math, so good luck. (3 TP)
Swan Song (Hero Tech): When it seems like all that's left is to fall, you must never give up hope! When you defeat an enemy while Downed, remove Downed from yourself and restore 1 HP.

Attack Classes

\\\ Profaner (Dark Blast) ///
A simple all-offense class, Profaners channel a connection to the Profound Darkness to single-handedly wipe entire armies off the field with powerful antiphoton techniques. Anyone can use the Profaner effectively, if they're willing to pay the price for their power. Inspired by the Phantasy Star Online 2 mechanic "Dark Blast."
\\ Max HP 300, 3 TP per turn //
P [P-ATK 110 / P-DEF 80 / P-ACC 110 / P-EVD 130]
R [R-ATK 100 / R-DEF 90 / R-ACC 100 / R-EVD 120]
M [M-ATK 110 / M-DEF 90 / M-ACC 110 / M-EVD 120]
Skills: Falz Gear, Hunger for Destruction, Dust Sweep, Ortho Sabarta, Convergent Ray, Tyrant Strike, Infinity Rush, Punishment Knuckle, White-Gold Rose, Black Hole, Violence Step, Red Queen Arrival, Pure White Darkness, Boosted CT
Falz Gear (Dark Blast): Gain the "Antiphoton" Job Gauge (max 250). This gauge fills when inflicting damage. When the gauge overfills, or when an enemy is defeated, create a Shadow Photon item and clear Exhaust.
Hunger for Destruction (Dark Blast): Surpass your limits. Let nothing stop you. If you have no Shadow Photons, you can pay a Shadow Photon cost by inflicting yourself with Defend Down instead.
Dust Sweep (Dark Blast): 100%, M25. A spherical energy field that wipes away foot soldiers. Against mooks, -5 CT. (3E, 1 TP)
Ortho Sabarta (Dark Blast): 100%, M40. Homing ice lances through the air. Exhaust: target all enemies. (1E, 1 TP)
Convergent Ray (Dark Blast): 90%, M50. A giant purple laser. Pierces guard, block, and damage-reduction effects. (2E, 1 TP)
Tyrant Strike (Dark Blast): 110%, P40. A diving jumpkick. On a crit, inflicts Defend Down. (1E, 1 TP)
Infinity Rush (Dark Blast): 80%, 1-6x P10. Repeatedly attack until you miss. If you reach five hits, the sixth becomes a P30 finisher. (1E, 1 TP)
Punishment Knuckle (Dark Blast): 95%, P40. An overwhelming single punch. Add Delay 1 up to six times to add 5 power for each. (1E, 1 TP)
White-Gold Rose (Dark Blast): Embody the Profound Darkness and welcome it into your mind. Consume a Shadow Photon. Until the end of this turn, your physical attacks gain +10% accuracy and your magical attacks gain +10 power. (0 TP)
Black Hole (Dark Blast): Photomagnetic vortex draws units close together. Consume a Shadow Photon, then choose three targets. Until the end of this turn, any effect that targets one now targets all of them.
Violence Step (Dark Blast): When targeted by an attack, consume a Shadow Photon. You're immune to that attack, and you gain Power Up for one turn.
Red Queen Arrival (Dark Blast): Call forth a corrupted ARKS mech to follow you instinctively. Gain the Red Queen status: the first time each turn you hit with an attack, add an extra 40-power attack of the same damage type to one of the skill's targets, at random.
Pure White Darkness (Dark Blast): [100%], P250, Delay 4. The ultimate technique of gold and black wings. Freezes a single unit in time-distortion and summons a set of four floating swords. The target can't react or interrupt until their next turn. (1E)
Boosted CT (Hero Property): Heroes are always the best at what they do. At the end of your turns, receive between 1 and 20 extra CT.
\\\ Bouncer (Jet Pursuit) ///
An elegant and stylish offense class, Bouncers use a rocket-polearm to launch wildly after enemies, striking down to propel themselves back up. They soar through the air with stored magic charge streaming behind them, striking stylish poses the whole time. Truly, these are the residents of the sky. Inspired by the traditional Kode class "Dragoon," and a few other things...
\\ Max HP 300, 3 TP per turn //
P [P-ATK 110 / P-DEF 90 / P-ACC 120 / P-EVD 130]
R [R-ATK 100 / R-DEF 80 / R-ACC 100 / R-EVD 130]
M [M-ATK 100 / M-DEF 90 / M-ACC 100 / M-EVD 120]
Skills: Rebound, Storm Chaser, Tetraseal Rush, Jetsweep Dive, Meteor Impact, Assist Powder, Blast Powder, Aerial Chase, Wyvern Wing Jump, Shooting Star, Extract Steal, Wiredrive Escape, Black Hot Victory, Power in Your Soul, Boosted CT
Rebound (Jet Pursuit): With each hit, you build glowing magical charge that streams behind you. Gain the "Combo" Job Gauge (max 20). When you attack and hit, increase Combo by 1 for each hit. When attacks miss you, increase Combo by 1. If all hits of your attack miss, reset Combo to 0.
Storm Chaser (Jet Pursuit): You're harder to hit when you're moving-- and you're always moving. While you have Delay or a Combo over 5, all Evade stats rise by 5.
Tetraseal Rush (Jet Pursuit): 85%, 4x P15. A rapid-fire spinning set of hits with both ends of the polearm. (1E, 1 TP)
Jetsweep Dive (Jet Pursuit): 90%, 3x P20. A triple-dive that pushes the target's evasion to its limits. If your spear doesn't catch them, your kick might. If this raises your Combo over 7, it adds an additional guaranteed hit that resets Combo and deals 5 * Combo. (1E, 1 TP)
Meteor Impact (Jet Pursuit): 110%, P??, Delay 4. A single strike straight through the foe, the only light your shining rocket blade. On hit, reset Combo; base power is 10 * Combo. On miss, keep Combo. (1E, 1 TP)
Assist Powder (Jet Pursuit): [100%], H??. Vent your magical charge into healing energy, getting your team back on their feet. Consume any amount of Combo, adding 10 power per point. (2A, 1 TP)
Blast Powder (Jet Pursuit): [100%], M??. Detonate your magical charge in a shining explosion so big it guarantees a hit. Consume any amount of Combo, adding 5 power per point. (1E, 1 TP)
Aerial Chase (Jet Pursuit): Dash after a retreating enemy, extending your chance to bounce one more time. If your last attack this turn missed, restore your combo. (0 TP)
Wyvern Wing Jump (Jet Pursuit): 95%, P40, Delay 2. Vault high into the air and come crashing down-- or prepare to rocket out of the way of an incoming attack! On hit, gain an additional 2 Combo. (1E, 1 TP)
Shooting Star (Jet Pursuit): Twirl in the air and strike a pose. Blasting your own theme music is optional. If you have at least 5 Combo, gain Hit Up. (1 TP)
Extract Steal (Jet Pursuit): Slip past the target, snagging a little of their power. Steal a buff from an enemy, or duplicate a buff from/to an ally. Once per turn cycle. (The ref will tell you if an effect will only be copied instead of stolen, or can't be copied at all.)
Wiredrive Escape (Jet Pursuit): Rocket out of harm's way at the last possible moment. When you're hit while Delaying, reset your Delay and dodge the hit.
Black Hot Victory (Jet Pursuit): Push your polearm into overdrive, blasting wildly through the air. Until the end of this turn, your Combo can't go down. At the end of the turn, reset Combo.
Power in Your Soul (Jet Pursuit): Embody elegant combat, grinding and wall-running with flair and style. Gain the "Funky Jumper" status: When your Combo would reset, it decreases by 3 instead.
Boosted CT (Hero Property): Heroes are always the best at what they do. At the end of your turns, receive between 1 and 20 extra CT.
\\\ Scion (Successor Arts) ///
A versatile, agile offensive class that switches fluidly between physical and ranged attacks. Drawing out the Scion's full potential requires carefully thinking through your combo tree, but a truly skilled one can be both devastating and untouchable. Inspired by the PSO2 Scion Classes "Hero" and "Luster."
\\ Max HP 300, 3 TP per turn //
P [P-ATK 110 / P-DEF 100 / P-ACC 110 / P-EVD 110]
R [R-ATK 110 / R-DEF 100 / R-ACC 110 / R-EVD 120]
M [M-ATK 100 / M-DEF 100 / M-ACC 100 / M-EVD 110]
Skills: High Voltage, Neutral Rapid, Scion Time, Fomel Blast, Zandi Cut, Baran Buster, Scion Gear, Leaping Dodge, Step Evade, Part Break, Star's Successor, Ace of Scions, Boosted CT
High Voltage (Successor Arts): Those flashy explosions aren't just for show-- the more you hit, the more charged photons surround you, waiting to be put to use. Gain the "Voltage" Job Gauge (max 10). When you deal damage, increase Voltage by 1. When you reach max Voltage, gain Power Up. When you're Downed, reset Voltage to 0.
Neutral Rapid (Successor Arts): 80%, 3x R20. Hold down the trigger and fill the air with weak neutral-photon shots. Exhaust: Accuracy +30%. (1-3E, 1 TP)
Scion Time (Successor Arts): A stylish snap of the fingers ends one combo, and supercharges the next. Consumes all Voltage (at least 5). Clears Exhaust, and grants an extra [5x Voltage] base damage to your next skill. (0 TP)
Fomel Blast (Successor Arts): 100%, P30. A sudden backstep into swirling shadow, chaining into a lunge wreathed in flame. If last skill was physical: if you have 3 or more Voltage, power +20. If last skill was ranged: targets all enemies. (1E, 1 TP)
Zandi Cut (Successor Arts): 80%, P40. A single lightning-quick swing of the gunslash, projecting a blade-slash of wind. If last skill was physical: accuracy +30%. If last skill was ranged: power +30. (1E, 1 TP)
Baran Buster (Successor Arts): 90%, R35. A reflecting shot that splits into more prismatic colors with each bounce. If last skill was physical: inflict your choice of Stat Down. If last skill was ranged: inflict Poison. (1E, 1 TP)
Scion Gear (Successor Arts): Release built-up photon charge in a five-pointed ARKS-star flash. Exhaust. Your next Scion attack this turn gains both conditional benefits and either +25 power or an additional target. (0 TP)
Leaping Dodge (Successor Arts): Seemingly-useless skill ring catapults you into the air, but provides iframes. Become Invincible for 3 ticks. Once per battle. (3 TP)
Step Evade (Successor Arts): Backflip through an attack's hitbox, iframes delivering you safely to the other side. Once per turn, when you'd be hit by an attack, Exhaust and dodge the attack entirely.
Part Break (Successor Arts): Shatter a piece of the target's armor, knocking them out of their animation. Reduce target's CT by 3 * your current Voltage, then set your Voltage to 0. (Targeting the unit starting their turn does not prevent the turn from starting.)
Star's Successor (Successor Arts): Surpass the heroes who came before you, and carve a path for those who'll follow. Clear Exhaust, then gain the Successor status: when you have less than maximum Voltage and you'd normally become Exhausted, gain 1 Voltage instead.
Ace of Scions (Successor Arts): Convert a Photon Blast into an overpowering aura of light, readying a devastating combo finisher. Gain the Ace of Scions status: your next Scion Time or Part Break treats your Voltage as double the maximum.
Boosted CT (Hero Property): Heroes are always the best at what they do. At the end of your turns, receive between 1 and 20 extra CT.
\\\ Decider (Vanguard Pilot) ///
A powerful and heavy offensive class, Deciders take to the field in a Vanguard, a three-meter-tall mech suit with a shoulder-mounted railgun originally built for military use. Experts at dealing damage and controlling the battlefield, these heavy machines can dance and weave through the front lines, blazing open a path for their forces. Inspired by the Xbox game "Steel Battalion."
\\ Max HP 300, 3 TP per turn //
P [P-ATK 110 / P-DEF 130 / P-ACC 110 / P-EVD 90 ]
R [R-ATK 110 / R-DEF 120 / R-ACC 110 / R-EVD 90 ]
M [M-ATK 100 / M-DEF 120 / M-ACC 100 / M-EVD 90 ]
Skills: Vanguard Tank, HSD Railgun, Magdump, Call Supply Drop, Smoothbore Rifle, Chaingun, Forecast Shot System, Shoulder Charge, Plasma Torch, Overdrive, Battery Drive, Boost Dash, AS-MIS, Prominence, Cutter Boom, Boosted CT
Vanguard Tank (Vanguard Pilot): You pilot a mech that chews through power and ammunition, fast. Gain the "Battery" Job Gauge (max 100, starts at 50). Battery charges over time, at 1% per tick. In addition, enter battle with 4 clipazines.
HSD Railgun (Vanguard Pilot): 100%, R100, Delay 4. A single shot so powerful the magnetic field disrupts monitors and sensors for kilometers around. Exhaust and consume 30% Battery. (1E, 1 TP)
Magdump (Vanguard Pilot): Rapidly reload to cancel the recoil animation. Consume one clipazine and clear Exhaust. Can be used multiple times per turn... but purists would call that an exploit. (0 TP)
Call Supply Drop (Vanguard Pilot): Dial the supply ship Argus and request a chopper with additional ammo. In 20 ticks, refresh your clipazines. Only one supply drop per player can be in progress at a time. (1 TP)
Smoothbore Rifle (Vanguard Pilot): 95%, 1-3x R25. One to three shots of massive 315mm ammunition. Choose 1, 2, or 3 shots. Single-shot mode gains +15% accuracy and inflicts Defend Down on hit. Triple-shot mode Exhausts you. (1E, 1 TP)
Chaingun (Vanguard Pilot): 90%, R10. A heavy gatling gun that clears out objectives. Inflicts CT damage equal to damage. (1E/AE, 1 TP)
Forecast Shot System (Vanguard Pilot): A 2nd-gen system that automatically predicts the target's movement. Consume 10% Battery. Next attack this turn has +15% accuracy and ignores reactions. (0 TP)
Shoulder Charge (Vanguard Pilot): [75%]. Rush the target and collide face-first, attempting to knock them down. Reduces target's CT by 1d10+10. (1 TP)
Plasma Torch (Vanguard Pilot): 80%, P60. The last-resort weapon of every VT: a point-blank torch that can core straight through a cockpit. On hit, inflicts Evade Down. (1E, 1 TP)
Overdrive (Vanguard Pilot): Chew through fuel for more power. Consume 10% Battery, then add 20 power and "Delay: 3 ticks" to your next attack. (0 TP)
Battery Drive (Vanguard Pilot): A moment's tactical retreat to let the reactor recharge can mean winning the war. Gain 3d4+6 Battery. (1 TP)
Boost Dash (Vanguard Pilot): Dash into an ally and knock them out of the way. Once per turn, when an ally is hit by an attack, you take the hit instead. Clear Exhaust.
AS-MIS (Vanguard Pilot): A homing missile system that can be defused with chaff, but doing that forces the target to split their attention. Consume 20% Battery. Choose either "attack" or "non-attack" skills. If the unit starting their turn uses the type of skill you selected during this turn, they take a 95%, R50 attack.
Prominence (Vanguard Pilot): Requisition the deployment of a special next-generation reactor. Gain the "Prominence" status: Battery gain per tick becomes 4%, and your Railgun shots have no base delay.
Cutter Boom (Vanguard Pilot): 100%, P250. Swing free a whole-ass chainsaw on an extending lance. On a miss, does not consume your LB charge, but reuse costs minimum 1 TP. (1E)
Boosted CT (Hero Property): Heroes are always the best at what they do. At the end of your turns, receive between 1 and 20 extra CT.

Support Classes

\\\ Arcanimist (Lost Arcanima) ///
A support class that provides buffs, healing, and shielding to their allies, Arcanimists are the last bearers of the art of controlling floating magitek pylons called distaves, thought lost to history during the Seventh Umbral Calamity. Inspired by what information exists of the unimplemented FFXIV 1.0 class "Arcanist."
\\ Max HP 300, 3 TP per turn //
P [P-ATK 90 / P-DEF 120 / P-ACC 100 / P-EVD 100]
R [R-ATK 90 / R-DEF 120 / R-ACC 100 / R-EVD 100]
M [M-ATK 100 / M-DEF 130 / M-ACC 120 / M-EVD 100]
Skills: Gifted Arcanima, Create Distaff, Deploy Distaff, Animate Distaff, Recall Distaff, Spindle Winding, Upgrade Distaff, Raise, Arcane Needle, Interject, Gear Bloom, Arcanimistic Eukrasia, Warp and Weft, Boosted CT
Gifted Arcanima (Lost Arcanima): A student of a lost art that unifies magic, technology, and arcane geometry. Gain the "Anima" Job Gauge (max 7, starts at 3). Consume 2 Anima to reduce the TP cost of a non-damaging skill by 1. In addition, start battle with two undeployed distaves.
Create Distaff (Lost Arcanima): Weave a fresh magitek pylon out of thin air. It floats beside you, waiting to be deployed. Creates a single undeployed distaff. 1 Anima: Make this 0 TP. (1 TP)
Deploy Distaff (Lost Arcanima): Imbue a distaff with a sigil, then send it forth to apply it in a bloom of arcane patterns. Deploys a single undeployed distaff to a target without one. Choose one Stat Up or Stat Down status, and apply it to that target for 20 ticks. (1T, 1 TP)
Animate Distaff (Lost Arcanima): Spins a deployed distaff's gears, remotely reactivating it. Choose a deployed distaff. It either reapplies its status, applies a new status, or heals its target (H50, +25 for each upgrade). 1 Anima: Add an additional target. (1T, 1 TP)
Recall Distaff (Lost Arcanima): Recalls a deployed distaff to your hand. Remove one of your distaves from a unit and clear any buffs it provided. Gain 1 Anima. (0 TP)
Spindle Winding (Lost Arcanima): Creates a shining weave of threads around a target, entangling incoming blows. Choose a deployed distaff. Grants Barrier<50> to that target. (1T, 1 TP)
Upgrade Distaff (Lost Arcanima): Unfolds extra wings from a deployed distaff, increasing potency. Upgrades both the level of the status effect and healing power (+25 per level, upgradable twice). (1 TP)
Raise (Lost Arcanima): Cross-class skill from Conjurer, probably. Restores life to a Downed ally, setting them to 25% HP. Spend an additional TP to restore them to 100% HP. (1 TP)
Arcane Needle (Lost Arcanima): M20. Snags a distaff out of the air and swings it, firing a bolt of light. If you have an undeployed distaff, double base power. The first time each turn you cast this, gain 1 Anima. (1E, 1 TP)
Interject (Lost Arcanima): Launch a distaff into the ground at an ally's feet. Consume 1 Anima. Deploy a distaff.
Gear Bloom (Lost Arcanima): H40. Detonate a deployed distaff to heal the ally taking shelter behind it. At any time, destroy a distaff, and heal its target. Any statuses persist. 1 Anima: Move another deployed distaff first. (1E)
Arcanimistic Eukrasia (Lost Arcanima): The culmination of magitek: your distaves fire on all cylinders. Grants Eukrasia status: when you activate distaves, they're always treated as max level.
Warp and Weft (Lost Arcanima): Lift your allies by the very threads of fate, if you have to. This turn, your healing has doubled base power and can bring Downed allies back to life.
Boosted CT (Hero Property): Heroes are always the best at what they do. At the end of your turns, receive between 1 and 20 extra CT.
\\\ Wrathguard (Four Quadrants) ///
A defensive class, the Wrathguard fights with massive greatsword and fist interchangeably, knocking aside their opponents' blows. With their powerful reactions, they often fight almost as much outside their turn as they do on their own turn, defending by interrupting and intercepting enemy attacks. Inspired (loosely) by 14th century German longsword techniques.
\\ Max HP 300, 3 TP per turn //
P [P-ATK 110 / P-DEF 130 / P-ACC 110 / P-EVD 90 ]
R [R-ATK 100 / R-DEF 130 / R-ACC 100 / R-EVD 90 ]
M [M-ATK 100 / M-DEF 120 / M-ACC 100 / M-EVD 90 ]
Skills: Blade and Fist, Time of the Hand, Time of the Foot, Guard Chase, Rising Strike, Pommel Strike, Deep Breath, Prepare Reaction, Nachreisen, Mordschlag, Zornhau, False Edge, Salute, True Art of the Blade, Wild Rauschen, Boosted CT
Blade and Fist (Four Quadrants): You fight with both sword and open palm, where your life depends on your skill and no move is illegal. Gain the "Breath" Job Gauge (max 250). When you take damage, increase the gauge by the damage value.
Time of the Hand (Four Quadrants): You've mastered tempo and flow in hand-to-hand combat. When an enemy uses an ability that costs more than 1 TP, for each extra TP, you can react an additional time.
Time of the Foot (Four Quadrants): That which you can't dodge, you can block behind your massive greatsword. While Exhausted, increase all Defense stats by 15.
Guard Chase (Four Quadrants): Leap in front of an attack, attempting to knock the blow aside with your massive sword. When an enemy attacks, choose a target who's been hit. Roll [50%]: on success, the hit is cancelled; on failure, you take the hit instead.
Rising Strike (Four Quadrants): [75%]. Swing up and catch the opponent with the reverse edge of your blade. When an enemy attacks you, Exhaust. On hit, cancel the attack, still consuming resources; the target can't use that skill this turn.
Pommel Strike (Four Quadrants): [100%]. A desperate rushing blow to the face that briefly stuns the target. Use only while Exhausted. When an enemy attacks you, inflict Power Down for one tick.
Deep Breath (Four Quadrants): [100%], H??. Fall back a step and take a breather, healing yourself or an ally. Reset your Breath; power equals Breath/2. If Breath was 100 or higher, clear Exhaust on yourself. (1T, 1 TP)
Prepare Reaction (Four Quadrants): Stand perfectly still, and let your enemy deliver themselves straight onto your blade. Gain Readied: you can react one additional time to the same trigger, as long as you use a different Reaction. This effect clears at the start of your next turn. (1 TP)
Nachreisen (Four Quadrants): 95%, P50. A "traveling after" strike that hits the target immediately after their own blow. If the target's CT is 35 or less, a miss becomes a hit, and a hit becomes a critical. (1E, 1 TP)
Mordschlag (Four Quadrants): 110%, P40. A killing blow; in the Wrathguard tradition, a leg-sweep into a cross-side slash. (1E, 1 TP)
Zornhau (Four Quadrants): 100%, P70. One of the five master strikes: a wide overhand sweep. To use, either Exhaust, or consume Breath and add Breath/4 power. (1E, 1 TP)
False Edge (Four Quadrants): That was just a feint, to force them off-balance! If you've missed this turn, increase the base accuracy of your next attack by 20. (0 TP)
Salute (Four Quadrants): Toss your opponent a salute, whether it's the full hand or just one finger. Add Hit Up for two ticks to yourself and one ally. (S+1A, 1 TP)
True Art of the Blade (Four Quadrants): Perfect focus, perfect skill. This is what you've been training for. Let all else melt away. Gain the "True Art" status: steadily gain 5 Breath each tick.
Wild Rauschen (Four Quadrants): 120%, 3x P80. A rush of strikes that send blade-on-blade sparks racing through the air. Increase your Breath by half the damage inflicted. (1E)
Boosted CT (Hero Property): Heroes are always the best at what they do. At the end of your turns, receive between 1 and 20 extra CT.
\\\ Arbiter (Calling Card) ///
A versatile and highly-tactical support class, the Arbiter is the latest student in a long tradition of RCP technicians. They draw "calling cards" from the callscript, summoning allies from distant worlds and sending them leaping into the fray at just the right moment. A skilled Arbiter always has the perfect card up their sleeve. Inspired by standard arena classes like "RCP Tech" and "SeeD."
\\ Max HP 300, 3 TP per turn //
P [P-ATK 100 / P-DEF 110 / P-ACC 100 / P-EVD 120]
R [R-ATK 100 / R-DEF 110 / R-ACC 100 / R-EVD 100]
M [M-ATK 100 / M-DEF 110 / M-ACC 100 / M-EVD 120]
Skills: Summon Disc, Redraw, Summon Assist, Summon Defender, Summon Supporter, Cross-Summon, Destiny Draw, Polymerize, Call Spirit, Call Supporter, Call Striker, Force Reroll, Wayfinder, Climax Scramble, Gate-Crosser, Boosted CT
Summon Disc (Calling Card): Throw wide the gates, and bring forth allies across worlds! Enter battle with five random callcards, and at the end of your turn, draw until you have five.
Redraw (Calling Card): What the hell is Kamen Rider? Who ARE you people?! Discard any number of callcards. Then, for each card you discarded, reveal one card, and choose only one of these revealed cards to keep. (0 TP)
Summon Assist (Calling Card): 90%. Consume a callcard to open up a foe for one of your allies. Inflicts Defend Down or Dodge Down for 8 ticks, rank based on Dangerous rank. (1 TP)
Summon Defender (Calling Card): [100%]. Consume a callcard to protect an ally. Applies Barrier<20/rank>, Defend Up, or Dodge Up (8 ticks), rank/strength based on Selfless rank. (1A, 1 TP)
Summon Supporter (Calling Card): 90%. Consume a callcard to boost an ally or delay an enemy. Adds or subtracts CT based on Tactical rank (10/rank up to 3, 5/rank past that). (1T, 1 TP)
Cross-Summon (Calling Card): A double summon that can draw out the power of relationships. Consume two callcards, adding their stats and acting based on highest combined stat. If the characters are similar or connected, the effect may be more powerful... or unpredictable. (1 TP)
Destiny Draw (Calling Card): You can't entirely defy luck... but you can make it bend. Reveal three unique cards and pick one to keep. Once there are at least five series with ten draws each, you may choose a specific series to draw from. (1 TP)
Polymerize (Calling Card): Consume two held callcards, to create... some combination of the two? Results are highly variable. (1 TP)
Call Spirit (Calling Card): Your favorite ally always returns to you. Choose a card you've used this turn, then set a Delay equal to its total rank * 2. When the Delay resolves, instantly draw that card. (1 TP)
Call Supporter (Calling Card): When an ally's turn starts, consume a callcard to have them act based on random highest stat. Selfless: Heal the target. Dangerous: Fight alongside the target, applying Assist. Tactical: Apply 1-tick Power Up.
Call Striker (Calling Card): When an enemy's turn starts, consume a callcard to have them act based on random highest stat. Selfless: Apply Barrier to yourself. Dangerous: Attempt to interrupt the target ([20%] per rank chance of TP -1). Tactical: Apply 1-tick Power Down.

\\\ Correspondent (Red Science) ///
An esoteric and strange support class, the Correspondent composes essays of flame in a searing language. Correspondents can twist and tweak skills of both allies and enemies as they're cast, but require careful planning and strategy to make the most of their odd skillset. Don't think too many sigils at once, or your hair might start to smolder! Inspired by the Fallen London profession "Correspondent."
\\ Max HP 300, 3 TP per turn //
P [P-ATK 100 / P-DEF 90 / P-ACC 100 / P-EVD 120]
R [R-ATK 100 / R-DEF 90 / R-ACC 100 / R-EVD 120]
M [M-ATK 120 / M-DEF 100 / M-ACC 120 / M-EVD 110]
Skills: Artisan Studies, Sketch, Judgement, Red Medicine, Write, Leaden Study, Inscribe, Red Miracle, Law of the Void, Boosted CT
Artisan Studies (Red Science): You pen letters and essays in the language of flame. You can hold sigils in your mind: base accuracy (not including brackets), base power, element, damage type (not including healing), a single tag, or targeting. While you hold more than three sigils in your mind, gain indefinite Burn status.
Sketch (Red Science): Steal inspiration, and capture part of the essence of a skill in your mind. Once per turn, when a skill is used, you can copy any single sigil from it. If the skill is targeting you, Sketch's cooldown doesn't apply. (Sigil copying is evaluated after applying all other modifiers. You can't both Sketch and Inscribe or Write the same skill.)
Judgement (Red Science): 85%, M40. Write "to become fire rather than be burned" in the Correspondence, and produce a radiant blast of golden light. The only truly universal language is power. For each sigil applied, increase total power by 15. (1E, 1 TP)
Red Medicine (Red Science): [100%], H40. Shroud the target in soothing darkness and write the Correspondence for "everything in its place," declaring that their wounds shall heal. For each sigil applied, add a target or increase base power by 20. (1+A, 1 TP)
Write (Red Science): Weave multiple sigils into deep and subtle meaning. You're both scientist and scholar, after all. Apply any number of held sigils to your next skill. Can be used multiple times per turn. (0 TP)
Leaden Study (Red Science): Engrave a sigil onto a special tablet. Just the one, mind-- even lead can burn. Converts a held sigil into a Tablet containing that sigil. Tablets can be passed to other fighters, or used by Correspondents like any other sigil. Once per turn, fighters without Write can use a single Tablet alongside any skill for 0 TP. (1 TP)
Inscribe (Red Science): [75%]. Release a sigil at the perfect moment, overwriting their reality with yours. Forcibly write a single held sigil onto someone else's skill, as they use it. If successful, this consumes the held sigil, and replaces the equivalent base value on the target skill for this use.
Red Miracle (Red Science): You need no permission from physics or nature. Write what now shall be, and force the world to comply. Gain the Red Miracle status: you don't take the Burn status from holding too many sigils, Inscribe becomes [100%], and Inscribe can write multiple sigils onto the same skill.
Law of the Void (Red Science): Engrave a blazing law upon the field. Write a law that all units must follow until the end of battle; ref gets final say on what's permissible. If anyone violates the law, they take a M60 attack. You may change the law as a 0 TP action.
Boosted CT (Hero Property): Heroes are always the best at what they do. At the end of your turns, receive between 1 and 20 extra CT.
